30 Second CPS Test — Endurance Click Challenge

The 30 second click test is where casual clickers hit their wall. While the 5 second test is about burst speed and the 10 second test tests moderate endurance, the 30-second challenge demands genuine stamina. Most users will see their CPS drop by 20–40% between the first 5 seconds and the final 5 seconds. Maintaining consistency across all 30 seconds is the ultimate test of clicking technique and physical conditioning.

Why Try the 30 Second Click Test?

The 30-second format is ideal for identifying fatigue patterns. Use it alongside our how to click faster guide to understand where your speed drops and which technique works best over longer periods. Butterfly clicking tends to outperform jitter clicking at this duration because jitter exhausts arm muscles rapidly.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is a 30 second click test?
A 30 second click test measures your sustained clicking ability over 30 seconds, calculating your average clicks per second. It heavily tests endurance — most people experience significant CPS drop after the 10-second mark.
What is a good CPS for 30 seconds?
A good sustained CPS over 30 seconds is 5–7 CPS for casual users. Maintaining 8+ CPS for the full 30 seconds is excellent. Anything above 10 CPS for 30 seconds requires advanced technique and strong hand endurance.
