Drag Click Test — The Ultimate Drag Clicking Speed Tester

Welcome to the most accurate drag click test on the internet. Drag clicking is an advanced mouse technique that allows players to register a massive number of clicks per second by dragging their finger across the mouse button surface. Unlike jitter clicking or butterfly clicking, drag clicking exploits friction to bounce the mechanical switch dozens of times per swipe.

What is Drag Clicking?

Drag clicking is a technique where you drag your finger from the top to the bottom of a mouse button, causing friction that makes the switch register multiple clicks in a single motion. A skilled drag clicker can register 25 to 100+ clicks per second, making it the highest-CPS technique available.

How to Drag Click — Step-by-Step

  1. Choose the Right Mouse: You need a mouse with a rough or textured button surface and a responsive mechanical switch. Popular choices: Glorious Model O, Razer DeathAdder, Roccat Kone Pure Ultra.
  2. Position Your Finger: Place your finger near the top edge of the mouse button at a 30–45 degree angle.
  3. Apply Consistent Pressure: Drag your finger downward with moderate, consistent pressure to create friction and make the switch bounce repeatedly.
  4. Practice the Motion: Use this drag click test to measure progress. Beginners start at 15–20 CPS and can improve to 30–50+ CPS.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is drag clicking?
Drag clicking is a technique where you drag your finger across a mouse button to create friction, forcing the switch to bounce and register multiple clicks per swipe. It can achieve 25–100+ CPS.
What CPS can you get from drag clicking?
Skilled drag clickers achieve 25 to 100+ clicks per second, far exceeding jitter or butterfly clicking. The exact CPS depends on your mouse, technique, and finger pressure.
What mouse is best for drag clicking?
Best mice have a textured button surface. Popular choices: Glorious Model O, Razer DeathAdder, Roccat Kone Pure Ultra. These allow the switch to bounce rapidly when dragged.
Does drag clicking damage your mouse?
Yes, drag clicking accelerates wear on the mouse button surface and mechanical switch. Use a mouse designed for high-intensity use if you plan to drag click frequently.
