Double Click Test — Check Your Mouse & Measure Speed

The double click test serves two purposes: it checks if your mouse is accidentally registering double-clicks when you only click once, and it measures your overall click speed (CPS). This is one of the most commonly searched tests in the clicking community because the "double-click bug" affects millions of gaming mice including popular models from Razer, Logitech, and SteelSeries.

What is the Double Click Bug?

The double-click bug occurs when a mouse switch's mechanical contacts bounce after a single press, registering as two clicks. This is called switch debounce failure. It is extremely common in mice after 6–18 months of heavy use. Symptoms include:

  • Files opening instead of selecting when you single-click in Windows Explorer.
  • Accidental item drops in games like Minecraft when placing blocks.
  • Unintended double-firing in competitive shooters.

How to Fix Double Clicking Mouse

  1. Increase Debounce Time: In your mouse software (Razer Synapse, Logitech G Hub, etc.), find the debounce or click latency setting and increase it to 10–20ms.
  2. Clean the Switch: Open the mouse and spray contact cleaner (like DeoxIT D5) into the switch. This often resolves the bouncing caused by oxidized contacts.
  3. Replace the Switch: Kailh GM 8.0 or Omron D2FC-F-7N(10M) switches can be soldered in as replacements for around $1–3 each.
  4. Use Windows Fix: Open Control Panel → Mouse → adjust the double-click speed slider to slower.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is a double click test?
A double click test checks if your mouse registers double-clicks correctly and measures how fast you can double-click per second. It helps diagnose the double-click bug common in gaming mice.
Why is my mouse double clicking on its own?
Unintended double-clicking is caused by a worn-out mouse switch (debounce failure). When the mechanical switch contacts bounce after a single press, it registers as two clicks. Common in older or heavily-used mice.
How do I fix a mouse that double clicks?
You can fix double clicking by increasing the debounce time in mouse software, cleaning the switch contacts with contact cleaner, or replacing the mouse switch entirely.
