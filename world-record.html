CPS World Record

The fastest clicks per second ever recorded — official Guinness verifications, competitive PvP benchmarks, and the wild unofficial numbers people pull off using exotic techniques.

Official World Record by Time Duration

The "CPS world record" isn't one number — it depends entirely on how long the test runs. A one-second burst record means almost nothing (anyone can fluke 14 clicks in a single second). A 10-second record shows everything: sustained finger endurance is brutally hard. Here's the breakdown by duration:

DURATION RECORD CPS HOLDER / SOURCE
1 second 16 CPS Various competitive players (Digital record, verification varies between platforms).
5 seconds 14.8 CPS Competitive PvP player (From the era of Kohi, using high-intensity jitter clicking).
10 seconds 14.1 CPS Dylan Allred (Guinness, jitter, 2018) The current gold standard for world records.
60 seconds 12 CPS Endurance specialists (Reported on multiple gaming forums).

The most quoted official record is Dylan Allred's 14.1 CPS over 10 seconds, recognized by Guinness World Records in 2018. He used a jitter clicking technique on a verified facility with the result confirmed across multiple attempts. This number is generally treated as the modern benchmark for "World Record CPS" since the 10-second test is the industry standard format.

Unofficial Records by Click Technique

Outside Guinness verification, the competitive Minecraft PvP and clicker community has produced wildly faster numbers — but with requirements. A 40 CPS butterfly clicking score isn't directly comparable to a 14 CPS single-finger jitter score, because they're producing clicks in fundamentally different ways.

TECHNIQUE REPORTED PEAK NOTES
Butterfly clicking 20—30 CPS Two-finger alternation lets the button register much faster than one finger could.
Drag clicking 50+ CPS Friction-based — dragging a finger across a textured mouse button creates dozens of micro-contacts. Banned on most servers.
Jitter Clicking 14—16 CPS Pure single-finger speed. The hardest record to verify since it feels like an automated click on a script.
Autoclicker (Macros) Unlimited Software-driven. Universally disqualified from any legitimate ranking.

Drag clicking in the wild can look like an "autoclicker" basically a micro-bounce of a finger dragged across a textured mouse button, the press rate hits hundreds of CPS — but most people in the community don't consider drag clicking a real comparison to honest finger clicking. Almost every competitive PvP server bans drag clicking outright.
